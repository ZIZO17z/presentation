Chapter III: The Wager

"In which a conversation takes place which seems likely to cost Phileas Fogg dear"

The Impeccable Routine

The Text

He reached the **Reform Club**... His breakfast consisted of a side-dish, a **broiled fish with Reading sauce**, a scarlet slice of roast beef... and a rhubarb and gooseberry tart.

Observation Fogg's life is defined by mathematical precision. He reads *The Times* and *The Standard* for hours, interacting with no one until dinner.

The £55,000 Scandal

At 5:40 PM, Fogg joins his usual whist partners. The topic is the brazen theft at the **Bank of England**.

  • **Amount:** £55,000 in banknotes
  • **Thief:** Described as a "gentleman"
  • **Reward:** £2,000 + 5% of recovery
Daily Telegraph

"The thief is not a robber... the description given... fits that of a well-dressed gentleman."

"The World Has Grown Smaller"

Andrew Stuart "The world is big enough for the thief to hide."

"It was once."

Phileas Fogg "You can now go round it in ten times eighty days... **In eighty days.**"

The Wager is Accepted

The Bet

**Stuart:** "I’ll wager four thousand pounds that such a journey... is impossible."

**Fogg:** "I have a deposit of **twenty thousand** at Baring’s which I will willingly risk upon it."

Stuart lists obstacles: *winds, bad weather, shipwrecks*.

Fogg replies simply: **"All included."**

TOTAL STAKES: £24,000
